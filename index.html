Yuan Chai

I am a doctoral candidate in Science and Technology of Aerospace Flight Dynamics Laboratory at Northwestern Polytechnical University, where I am advised by Prof. Jianjun Luo. My research is about game theory-based control for multi-agent system and its application in on-orbit servicing.

Recent Projects

On-orbit servicing refers to inspection, repair, refuel, upgrade and assembly in space. Considering the low cost and the short development of agents (e.g. microsatellite, robots), they are widely used in these on-orbit servicing missions. Recently, I have done some related projects in NWPU.

  • On-orbit assembly of truss structures

    • On-orbit assembly is an exciting path to achieve the broad configuration possibilities offered by a modular assembly system and sidestep some of the volume and mass constraints imposed by launching a monolithic satellite on a single launch vehicle. In this project, there are a team of transporting agents to transport substructures and a team of assembly robots to assembly these substructures. I worked on task allocation of transporting tasks, and planning and control for transporting tasks.

  • Takeover control of spacecraft

    • Spacecraft suffering from actuator failures can no longer provide normal service due to the loss of precise attitude adjustment ability, and would occupy valuable space debris or even become potential threats to spacecrafts that are under normal operation. In this project, several microsatellites are used to approach, fix on and takeover the failed spacecraft. I worked on the approach control stage and takeover control stage.
